<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Etihad Credit Bureau (AECB), with TDRA and Digital Dubai, has switched on a new tenant-screening flow. Landlords request a prospective tenant's credit score via the AECB app or web portal; the tenant authorises the share via UAE PASS, and the landlord sees the score plus an AI-driven cheque-clearance likelihood.
</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Flow: landlord initiates in AECB → tenant consents via UAE PASS → AECB returns credit score + AI cheque-clearance probability (predicting whether post-dated rent cheques will bounce). Fees and effective date not yet disclosed — verify with AECB before using for live tenancy decisions.
</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Dubai landlords had no national, consent-based tenant credit channel until now. A live AECB rail re-prices risk for tenanted-yield deals, sharpens the case for insisting on UAE PASS-verified tenants, and gives leasing brokers a new value-add at signing.
</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Dubai landlords screening prospective tenants, leasing brokers building higher-trust tenancy stacks, build-to-rent operators managing post-dated cheque portfolios, and yield-investors underwriting tenanted purchases.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Dubai realty regains momentum — DLD transactions +2% MoM in April, tenant enquiries +40%, rental inventory doubles as ~70% of listings cut ask ~10%</h2>
<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Eight weeks into the regional conflict, Dubai sales enquiries are +11%, tenant enquiries +40%, rental stock has doubled and ~70% of listings have already adjusted ask by ~10%.</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Betterhomes and Bayut/Dubizzle Property data, published by Khaleej Times on 17 May, show DLD transactions rose ~2% MoM in April, sales enquiries up 11%, tenant enquiries up 40%, and rental inventory more than doubled from ~1,000 to ~2,200 units since early March.
</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
~70% of rental listings cut ask by ~10%; Bayut/Dubizzle reports platform engagement already 104–108% of 2025 levels. Apartment intent leads in JVC, Business Bay, Downtown and Marina; villa intent in Dubai Hills, Arabian Ranches 3, Dubai South and DAMAC Lagoons.
</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Underwrite Dubai rentals on a softer landlord stance: supply doubled and 70% of landlords repriced within eight weeks, compressing rent-comp tables fast. Sales-side, the +11%/+40% Mar→Apr enquiry curve argues buyers are returning but with negotiation leverage.
</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Dubai landlords revisiting rent asks, agency leasing teams chasing the 40% intent uplift, and buy-side investors triangulating where to deploy as the market re-engages.
</p>
