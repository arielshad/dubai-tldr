<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Dubai Office Rents Post Double-Digit Growth Across All Tiers — JLL Q1 2026</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Grade B is the new Grade A: Grade B +23.4%, Grade A +19%, Prime +17.2% YoY as prime vacancy locks at 0.7%.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
JLL's Q1 2026 UAE Real Estate Market Dynamics report shows Dubai office rents surging double-digits across every tier — Grade B +23.4%, Grade A +19%, Prime +17.2% YoY. Prime vacancy is at a near-zero 0.7%, while citywide vacancy nudged to 7.3% on new deliveries.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Dubai's 101.1M sqft office inventory has almost no prime space left — tenants locked out of DIFC / SZR / Business Bay prime stock are cascading into Grade B, driving Grade B's outsized +23.4% jump. Renewals climbed +11.2% YoY while new contracts fell -7.7%, confirming existing occupiers are staying put.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the first hard Q1 2026 number from a tier-1 advisor. Landlords of Grade A/B in Business Bay, DIFC fringe and SZR can push at renewal; occupiers should expect 15–25% increases on rollover and consider locking 5+ year leases now while supply lag persists.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Commercial landlords pricing renewals, asset managers underwriting office cap rates, brokers running corporate tenant rep, and occupiers about to roll a Q3/Q4 2026 lease.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Europe's largest commercial landlord locks in 19 branded-residence units for staged 2027+ resale — the largest publicly disclosed debt facility against a Dubai branded-residence portfolio.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Emirates NBD Capital and Czech/Luxembourg-listed CPI Property Group (CPIPG) closed an AED 367.3M (~EUR 86M) loan facility on 21 May, secured against 19 ultra-prime Dubai residences across Bvlgari The Lighthouse, Casa Canal, One Canal, and Mr. C Residences Downtown — 15 still under construction, 4 completed.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Loan proceeds cover CPIPG's developer deferred-payment obligations through 2026–2027. After each building completes, CPIPG plans phased divestment marketing units to European, Asian and wider-MEA buyers — making CPIPG a motivated, financed-against-completion seller rather than a long-hold owner.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Confirms ultra-prime branded residences remain bankable collateral at UAE's largest lender, and that at least one major European institutional holder is now positioning to liquidate completed luxury inventory — adding 19 verifiable secondary units to the Jumeirah Bay / Dubai Water Canal / Downtown corridor.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Bulk-deal brokers wanting to be inside CPIPG's 2027 divestment cadence, HNW buyers willing to bid against an institutional seller, and lenders sizing up branded-residence collateral coverage.
