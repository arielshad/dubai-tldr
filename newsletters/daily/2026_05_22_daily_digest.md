<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Colliers Q1 2026 UAE report: Dubai apartment handovers top 10,000 for second straight month + 65,000-unit pipeline still to come</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">First Q1 supply read from a tier-1 consultancy: Dubai handovers keep clearing 10K apartments/month, but the 65K+12.5K still-to-come pipeline is what every rent-and-resale underwrite hinges on.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Colliers International's UAE Real Estate Market Report for Q1 2026 puts Dubai apartment handovers above 10,000 units/month for the second straight month, with ~1,900 villas delivered — while 65,000 apartments and 12,500 villas remain in the 2026 pipeline through year-end.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Apartment rents grew +2% QoQ and average sale prices kept trending upward across all asset classes, but completed-unit transaction volumes contracted QoQ through March — office stock was the standout performer on a shortage of Grade-A supply.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
65K apartments still to land into a market where ready-flat transactions are contracting means sell-side leverage continues to drift to buyers on stale stock; villa supply is comparatively tight (12.5K through year-end), so villa price firmness should hold while apartments soften.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Sell-side brokers underwriting ready-flat resale leverage, buyer reps comping villa scarcity, asset managers watching Dubai office Grade-A absorption, and analysts triangulating Colliers vs ValuStrat vs Property Monitor on the May 2026 inflection.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">If even half of the predicted 30% shutdown rate lands, Dubai's broker headcount per 100K compresses toward London's by year-end — and small-agency listings, leads and mandates are about to consolidate.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
An Arabian Business exclusive projects that up to 30% of Dubai agencies active earlier this year may stop operating within the next five to six months, exposed by the post-March 2026 correction after a six-year boom.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Dubai now has ~1,000 brokers per 100,000 residents vs London's 176, and smaller agencies are already approaching larger ones for acquisitions and mergers — described by executives as "four or five pay cycles" away from closure.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
For sellers, counter-party broker risk is real in Q3/Q4 — prioritise larger DLD-licensed brokerages for open mandates. For buyers, small-agency closeout listings may be where genuine motivated supply surfaces as agencies wind down.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Brokers stress-testing firm runway, agency owners evaluating M&A vs wind-down, sellers checking mandate counter-parties, and proptech teams comping broker density against global cities.
