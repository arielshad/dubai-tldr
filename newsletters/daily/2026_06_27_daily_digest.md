May 2026 residential sales fell 42% MoM to AED 22B from April, and 52% off February's AED 46B peak — but Grade A locations 'have not corrected,' citywide prices declined less than 5% in three months, and Grade B softened roughly 10%.

Sajwani argues 'the big liquidity spike will come in September' as Eid + summer travel + war-pause delays unwind. AHS itself closed the AED 1B Shangri-La Dubai acquisition in May at the unchanged January valuation.

For HNW buyers, 'Grade A hasn't corrected' is the directional read: the discount window is narrowing in prime. For Grade B sellers, the ~10% softness sets a realistic listing-cut band rather than panic discounting.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">May 2026 Dubai mortgage value AED 17.5B (+17% YoY) as cash sales pause — UAE loan book hits AED 1.45T</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Mortgaged-deal value beats cash flow as financed buyers lean in — May 2026 hits AED 17.5B mortgages on -45% deal count.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
AGBI's June 25 tape — sourced from Dubai Land Department, Betterhomes and UAE Central Bank — shows May 2026 mortgage value rose 17% YoY to AED 17.5B even as mortgaged-deal count fell 45% YoY, signalling fewer-but-bigger financed tickets. The UAE-wide mortgage-and-real-estate loan book reached AED 1.45T in April, up 11.5% YoY.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Cash deals dominated April at ~75% of transactions, but Dubai sales fell 22% YoY in April and 45% YoY in May — concentrated in the cash bucket. Mortgage flow held value because average ticket size scaled. Khaleej Times reported 1-year fixed rates at 3.75%, 2-year 3.78%, 3-year 3.95%, giving financed buyers a structural pricing edge.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Operators reading May as a market freeze get the signal wrong — financed-buyer demand absorbed inventory cash buyers passed on. The rate floor at 3.75% vs. ~6-7% rental yield means taking mortgaged inventory at present asks can beat rolling rent on the same unit.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Mortgage brokers, mid-market buyers timing rate locks, sellers calibrating cash-vs-financed offers, and institutional desks pricing the financed-buyer flow into 2H 2026.
</p>
