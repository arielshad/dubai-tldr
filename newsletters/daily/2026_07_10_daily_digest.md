<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Julius Baer drops Dubai to 14th on luxury-lifestyle index — dirham peg is doing the work, real estate still #1 ME HNWI asset class</h2>
<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Julius Baer drops Dubai seven rungs to 14th on its luxury-lifestyle index — a dollar-peg optical move, not a local price cut — with prime real estate still the #1 ME HNWI asset class at 23%.</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The Julius Baer 2026 Global Wealth &amp; Lifestyle Report puts Dubai at 14th in its cross-city luxury cost index, down from 7th last year. Real estate is called out as the #1 preferred asset class for Middle East HNWIs at 23%, well ahead of equities at 12%.
</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The report converts a 25-city basket of luxury spend into USD; Dubai's dirham is pegged to a weakening dollar, so the emirate looks cheaper even as local pricing holds. Global luxury goods rose 12.3% on average while Dubai's peg absorbs the lift, pushing it down the USD-priced index without any real local price fall.
</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The dirham peg is now an explicit brochure line for prime-residential agents targeting European and Asian HNWIs whose home currencies bought less this year. With real estate at 23% of ME HNWI preferred allocation, the flow story for Palm Jumeirah, Emirates Hills and Jumeirah Bay branded stock stays intact.
</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Luxury agencies pitching Palm Jumeirah / Emirates Hills / Jumeirah Bay to European and Asian HNWIs; branded-residence sales teams framing the currency angle; family offices comparing Dubai prime-residential to Zurich, Monaco, HK and Singapore.
</p>
