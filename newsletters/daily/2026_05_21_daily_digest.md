<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Colliers Q1 2026: Dubai apartment handovers top 10,000 for second straight month — 65K apartments still in 2026 pipeline</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">First Q1 supply read from a tier-1 consultancy: Dubai handovers keep clearing 10K apartments/month, but the 65K+12.5K still-to-come pipeline is what every rent-and-resale underwrite hinges on.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Colliers' UAE Real Estate Market Report for Q1 2026 (published 20-May-2026) is the first tier-1 consultancy print to quantify how much physical Dubai supply has actually been handed over this year. Dubai apartment handovers exceeded 10,000 units for the second consecutive month in Q1; roughly 1,900 villas were delivered; and ~65,000 apartments plus 12,500 villas remain in the 2026 pipeline.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Apartment rents grew +2% QoQ while villa rents held stable, completed-unit sales activity declined quarterly (accelerating into March), but average sale prices kept trending up. Office sales posted standout growth on a shortage of completed Grade-A stock.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the supply-side anchor every Dubai underwriter needs for 2026. Sellers face 65K apartments landing into a market where ready-flat transactions are contracting QoQ. Villa supply is comparatively tight (12.5K through year-end), so villa price firmness should persist while apartments soften.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Sell-side brokers underwriting ready-flat resale leverage, buyer reps comping villa scarcity, off-plan sales teams building handover-delay protection into contracts, and analysts triangulating Colliers vs ValuStrat vs Property Monitor reads on the May 2026 inflection.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Dubai 2025 REPI: residential +9.81%, villas +14.83%, offices +15.86% — the official benchmark the 2026 correction is measured against</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Official 2025 benchmark: villas +14.83%, offices +15.86% — the numbers ValuStrat's 'first quarterly contraction since 2020' is being measured against.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Dubai Data &amp; Statistics Establishment published its Real Estate Price Index for full-year 2025 on 19-May-2026. Residential closed +9.81% YoY with villas (+14.83%) running roughly 2× apartments (+7.38%); commercial closed +9.54% with offices the standout at +15.86%; hospitality +4.80%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
REPI is the closed-2025 base index against which Q1–Q2 2026 contractions get measured. ValuStrat already flagged the first quarterly residential contraction since 2020 in March (-3.8% QoQ) — that reading sits on top of this +9.81% full-year base.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
It puts a hard number on the 2025 base and confirms offices (+15.86%) outpaced everything residential except villas — validating the Business Bay and commercial-record stories from earlier in May. For sellers it is the strongest argument against discounting beyond 5–15% of peak.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Underwriters anchoring 2026 entry comps to a verifiable 2025 base, sellers defending sub-peak asks, office investors confirming the +15.86% reading, and economists tying Dubai's print to D33 / Dubai Real Estate Strategy 2033.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Arabian Business exclusive: up to 30% of Dubai brokerages may shut in 5–6 months — ~1,000 brokers per 100K residents vs London's 176</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">If even half of the predicted 30% shutdown rate lands, Dubai's broker headcount per 100K compresses toward London's by year-end — and small-agency listings, leads and mandates are about to consolidate.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
An Arabian Business exclusive (20-May-2026) projects that up to 30% of agencies active earlier this year may stop operating within the next five to six months — described as "four or five pay cycles" by industry executives. Dubai now has close to 1,000 brokers per 100,000 residents, against ~176 in London.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The post-March 2026 correction is exposing operators that scaled during the six-year bull run. Smaller agencies are already approaching larger ones to negotiate acquisitions, mergers or integrations to avoid closure — the Unique Properties / Alessandro Console 7-figure acquisition is a live precedent.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Industry structure shifts compound: if 30% of agencies actually close, mandate concentration accelerates and the largest brokerages gain pricing power on listing fees. For deal pipelines, the practical rule for the next two quarters is: assume your counter-party broker may not exist by Q4 unless they're on a top-20 DLD-licensed list.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Brokers stress-testing their firm's runway, agency owners evaluating M&amp;A vs wind-down, sellers checking who they signed a mandate with, buyers shopping closeout listings, and proptech sales teams comping the broker-density gap to other global cities.
</p>

Layouts run 2-BR (3,400–3,500 sqft), 3-BR (2,500–3,800 sqft) and 4-BR (3,900–4,500 sqft) with an exclusive residents-only beach, infinity pools, wellness areas, and a rooftop lounge.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Dubai Holding breaks ground on Lantana Hills — AED 680M modular contract for 390 townhouses at Dubai Science Park, H2 2027 + LEED Silver</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">390 3/4-BR family townhouses going up 85% off-site via modular construction — DHAM's Science Park land bank delivered to LEED Silver standard inside 18 months.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Dubai Holding Asset Management broke ground on Lantana Hills on 20-May-2026 and awarded Group AMANA the AED 680M ($185M) main contract. The product: 390 private 3- and 4-bedroom townhouses at Dubai Science Park (Al Barsha South), targeted for H2 2027 handover with LEED for Cities and Communities: Plan &amp; Design Silver Precertification.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Group AMANA is using DuBox (2,730 modular units) and DuPod (2,020 prefabricated bathroom pods), completing ~85% of construction off-site. The parties claim a 30% material-waste reduction and &gt;70% on-site safety improvement vs traditional methods — the most explicit handover-delay defence seen post the Fitch/Anarock 6–9-month warning.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Dubai Science Park townhouse supply has been thin; 390 units at LEED Silver with a 10-minute Dubai Hills Mall commute is a sizeable mid-prime addition. At AED 680M / 390 units, the ~AED 1.74M average per-unit construction cost is a useful benchmark for any townhouse underwrite in JVC, Al Furjan and Arjan over the next 12 months.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Mid-prime family buyers shortlisting townhouse stock, brokers building Al Barsha South inventory lists, competing developers benchmarking modular-construction economics, ESG / sovereign-linked capital scoring LEED-certified product, and analysts watching Dubai Holding's portfolio capex run-rate.
