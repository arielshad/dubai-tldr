<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">AGBI / LuxuryPriceDrops.com: Dubai sellers cut AED 1.7B across 2,800+ listings — AED 110M Jumeirah villa marked to AED 85M, Arabian Ranches villa cut 5 times to AED 7.4M</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Independent tracker logs AED 1.7B of Dubai asking-price cuts — secondary villas leading, off-plan a step behind.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
AGBI's 11 May report cites LuxuryPriceDrops.com, an independent scraper monitoring ~27,000 live Dubai listings, finding 2,800+ properties marked down a cumulative AED 1.7B ($463M) since the regional conflict started in late February. Co-founder Matias Dorta says the pace of reductions is not slowing, with 500+ new drops logged in the past week alone.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Secondary (ready) markdowns are running roughly 2× off-plan markdowns by count, suggesting tenant-owner sellers are repricing faster than developers can flex payment plans. The biggest single cut is a 4-bedroom Jumeirah villa from AED 110M to AED 85M (−23%); an Arabian Ranches villa has been cut five times from AED 8.5M to AED 7.4M and is still unsold.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the most credible third-party measurement of seller motivation that buyers in Dubai have right now. The dataset gives buyers a price-discovery anchor for negotiating off list prices in Jumeirah villas, Arabian Ranches, and mid-luxury Marina/Downtown stock.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Cash buyers hunting villa repricings, brokers running buy-side mandates, secondary sellers benchmarking their ask, and analysts pricing risk into Q2 2026 underwrites.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Emaar Q1 2026: AED 20.1B property sales (+22%), AED 3.5B net profit (+36%), AED 134.6B backlog as 10 new launches land before regional conflict spreads</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">The biggest Dubai developer printed AED 20B of sales in a quarter that ended with a regional war — backlog now AED 134.6B and 10 new launches in flight.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Emaar Properties' Q1 2026 print: sales at Emaar Development were AED 20.1B (+22% YoY); group revenue AED 12.4B (+23%); net profit ~AED 3.5B (+36%); revenue backlog AED 134.6B (+35%). The company also paid out AED 8.9B (100% of share capital) at its AGM and launched 10 new residential projects across its master-planned communities.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
AED 20.1B in Q1 sales is roughly AED 6.7B per month, well ahead of Emaar's Q1 2025 base. The AED 134.6B backlog implies ~6.7 quarters of forward revenue at the Q1 sales pace, and 10 new launches plug into existing master communities with 2–3 year handover horizons.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Emaar is the single biggest developer-side benchmark in Dubai. If Q1 sales are up 22% and backlog up 35% during a quarter that ended with a hot regional conflict, the "launches will dry up" thesis is wrong — and secondary sellers in Emaar communities must negotiate against fresh-launch payment plans.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Off-plan brokers tracking primary-market depth; resale sellers in Emaar communities benchmarking against new-launch ask; institutional analysts modelling Dubai supply 2027–28; lenders sizing developer-receivable backed financing.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">fäm Properties + DLD holding-period study: 69.9% of Dubai primary buyers since 2012 still own — 740,219 properties never resold, retention now matches London/NYC</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Three-quarters of Dubai's post-2012 buyers never sold — fäm + DLD say the flip cycle is gone and retention now matches London/NYC.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
fäm Properties, working with Dubai Land Department data, ran the longest public holding-period analysis Dubai has ever published: 1.1M+ transactions across 16 years. The headline finding is that 740,219 homes purchased since 2012 have never traded again — 69.9% of primary-market buyers and 61.1% of resale buyers still own the unit they bought.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Cohort analysis shows 42% of 2014 primary buyers, 53% of 2017 buyers, and 61% of 2022 buyers still hold their units — a steepening retention curve. The study attributes this to the Golden Visa programme, regulatory protections for off-plan buyers, infrastructure expansion, and the maturation of family-led demand.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
If retention is now structurally London/NYC-like, the resale pipeline is thinner than transaction volumes suggest — most owners aren't sellers, so distress supply is mostly motivated/forced rather than discretionary. For developers, the signal is that the post-handover market won't flood the resale book on day one.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Brokers building seller pitches, off-plan launch operators sizing post-handover supply risk, ready-market underwriters benchmarking liquidity, and analysts updating Dubai vs global hold-period models.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Danube's first villa-led masterplan brings a 1%-monthly furnished townhouse to the Academic City / DSO spillover zone, three years out from handover.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
On 10-May-2026 Sheikh Nahyan bin Mubarak Al Nahyan unveiled Greenz by Danube at a Coca-Cola Arena event attended by 7,000+ investors — Danube's first large-scale integrated master villa community, marketed as Dubai's first fully furnished master villa project. The mix covers 3- and 4-bedroom townhouses plus 5-bedroom semi-detached and twin villas with Dolce Vita Italian branded interiors.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

It converts an AED 3.5M villa underwrite into a fully furnished package at a location with 100,000+ nearby residents and professionals.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">DLD week 19 (May 4–10): AED 7.99B ex-land across 3,598 deals (−17% w/w) — Lumena books 37-office AED 1.17B institutional cluster, Eden House AED 49M ready flat</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">First weekly print since the May surge: AED 7.99B is still huge but down 17% w/w — Business Bay leads both off-plan and ready, partly because Lumena alone aggregated 37 offices for AED 1.17B inside the week.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Week-19 DLD synthesis covering 4–10 May 2026: AED 7.99B ex-land over 3,598 deals (down 17% value and 14% count from week 18). Off-plan held 68.7% of activity at AED 5.49B; ready stock AED 2.50B. Business Bay topped both books, and the headline institutional trade was Lumena by Omniyat clearing 37 offices for AED 1.17B.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The week-on-week drop is not a bearish signal — week 18 was distorted by the AED 1.02B single-day Lumena cluster on May 5. The normalised read: AED 7.99B / 3,598 deals = ~AED 2.22M average ticket, consistent with the YTD off-plan-heavy pattern.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
AED 7.99B ex-land says the May surge is durable, not a one-shot. Business Bay's consecutive dominance across both off-plan and ready ledgers compresses commercial yields and tightens negotiation room on existing Business Bay office blocks — 37 offices clearing AED 1.17B at Lumena is a hard comp.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Brokers writing weekly market notes; commercial buyers underwriting Business Bay office blocks; off-plan resale negotiators in Dubai Water Canal / Wadi Al Safa 3; analysts reconciling daily and weekly DLD prints.

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Betterhomes flags first signs of Dubai market stabilisation 67 days into regional conflict — off-plan share jumps to 76% but ~70% of rental units saw ~10% cuts in April</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">First post-conflict "pause is ending" read on Dubai: rental-side softening is real, off-plan share is climbing, three forward catalysts queued.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Betterhomes' May 7 monthly webinar finds Dubai showing "early signs of stabilisation" 67 days into the regional conflict, with the secondary market still subdued but rental enquiries and off-plan registrations accelerating. April transactions were up ~2% MoM; off-plan share hit 76% (+7 pts vs March).
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Tenant enquiries jumped ~40% in April. On the listing side, ~70% of rental properties saw landlords cut asking rent by just under 10%. Three forward catalysts flagged: removal of the AED 750K floor on the 2-year property-investor visa, the AED 34B Gold Line metro, and the UAE's OPEC exit.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the first agency-level "we can see a floor" read since the regional conflict began on ~2026-03-02, from one of the city's largest brokerages. For landlords, the 70% / 10% rental-reset number is a hard data point: pricing above that delta means your unit will sit.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Landlords pricing 2026 renewals, buyers underwriting yield in JVC/Marina/Business Bay, off-plan buyers worried about long-stop dates, and operators tracking when the "wait-and-see" pause breaks.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Khaleej Times: AED 4B+ remote-conveyancing flow at Metropolitan over 1,000+ deals as overseas buyers close Dubai property without travelling</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">AED 4B in fully-remote Dubai conveyancing in a year is hard evidence that the buyer funnel for overseas capital is now POA-first — useful both for brokers chasing inbound leads and for sellers underwriting their counterparty.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Khaleej Times surfaces year-over-year scale numbers from Metropolitan Conveyancing: AED 4B+ in remote transactions, 1,000+ deals, ~30% YoY growth in conveyancing demand since early 2026. Head of department Saneta Daova calls remote completion "essential in the current climate," particularly for European and UK clients.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Remote conveyancing runs on a notarised, apostilled power of attorney; escrow/source-of-funds compliance; DLD title transfer or Oqood registration handled in-person by the conveyancer/POA holder; and coordinated handover. AED 4B / 1,000+ deals = ~AED 4M average ticket — well above the citywide AED 3.11M resale average, consistent with a wealthier overseas buyer mix.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
For brokers, this confirms an inbound-lead motion that doesn't depend on flying buyers in — a reason to keep WhatsApp/Calendly funnels live for European and UK names instead of waiting for the next IPS roadshow. For sellers, it reframes counterparty risk: a remote buyer with a credible POA structure is closeable, but will negotiate harder on snagging.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Off-plan / luxury brokers servicing European and UK leads; sellers underwriting remote-buyer offers; conveyancers and tax advisors; anyone benchmarking the overseas share of Dubai resale flow.
</p>
