<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Dubai Golden Visa: bank guarantee replaces 50% cash upfront for AED 2M property route</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Off-plan and mortgage buyers can now collect a 10-year Golden Visa at contract signing — GDRFA accepts a bank guarantee instead of 50% cash paid up.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
GDRFA has formalised a rule change letting AED 2M Golden Visa applicants substitute a bank guarantee for the previous 50%-paid-in-cash test. Off-plan investors and mortgage buyers can now obtain residency at the point of sales-contract signing — effective February 2026 and formally gazetted 11 April 2026.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Instead of clearing 50% of the purchase price in cash, the buyer arranges a UAE-bank guarantee covering the full AED 2M threshold and submits it to GDRFA with the property contract and Oqood/title. The guarantee stays in force until ownership is fully paid up; cost is the bank's guarantee-issuance fee plus collateral.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The 50%-paid rule was the single biggest friction in the property–visa pipeline, effectively locking most off-plan buyers out of Golden Visa for years. Combined with recent GCC policy reforms, Dubai now has the most liquid property-visa stack in the region.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Off-plan investors on developer payment plans (60/40, 70/30, post-handover) who want immediate residency, plus mortgage-buying expats and overseas investors using AED 2M+ UAE-bank loans.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Dubai off-plan office sales hit record AED 3B in April 2026 — YTD already 104% above all of 2025</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Off-plan office demand just printed a fresh all-time high — Business Bay is doing &gt;90% of it and 2025's full-year number has already been beaten by 104% in four months.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Dubai's off-plan office sales hit AED 3B in April 2026 — the highest monthly value ever recorded for the segment — across 318 deals, almost double March's 182. Business Bay alone absorbed AED 2.8B of the total.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Year-to-date through April the segment has cleared AED 9.4B over 1,269 deals — already 104% above the AED 4.6B booked across all of 2025. Lumena and similar towers in Business Bay are driving the majority of the order book, with Q4 2029–Q3 2030 handovers dominating payment plans.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This confirms the off-plan-office story isn't a one-day spike but a sustained multi-quarter run concentrated in Business Bay. Secondary-market sellers should expect rising ask comps but also stiffer competition from launch-stage inventory priced off 2029+ handovers.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Commercial investors and family offices considering Business Bay office exposure; secondary-market sellers calibrating asks against launch prices; brokers underwriting yield against rising service charges and DLD fees.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">

Brookfield Properties will act as development and real-estate manager, with Alshaya establishing its new UAE HQ on-site.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Bloomberg framed this as Brookfield's first big-ticket Middle East foray since the regional conflict began — described internally as a long-horizon conviction call on regional fundamentals. Project cost and construction timeline have not been disclosed.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>

It also adds a Brookfield-managed mixed-use block to a community dominated by villa/apartment inventory, raising the bar for future office/retail comps in Dubai Hills.

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Office and apartment underwriters working Dubai Hills/MBR City; family offices benchmarking against Brookfield's institutional Dubai risk pricing; brokers pitching nearby ready or off-plan stock; Al Quoz/SZR Road landlords watching for new Grade-A supply signals.
