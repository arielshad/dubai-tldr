<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Emaar teases AED 200B Dubai mega-masterplan — 4.5M sqm GFA, ~150K residents, 5 character zones, 20-minute city</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Emaar's biggest-ever Dubai land bet — AED 200B, 150K residents, 4.5M sqm across five zones — teased ahead of an imminent full unveil.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Emaar's founder Mohamed Alabbar pre-announced a new AED 200B (~USD 54.4B) integrated urban district with five zones, ~150,000 residents and >4.5M sqm GFA — project name and location still undisclosed. It features swimmable lagoons, metro connectivity, Grade-A offices, 5-6BR villas and views of Burj Khalifa, Burj Al Arab and Palm Jumeirah from selected residences.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Built around the 20-minute city principle, five zones span a business hub, urban district, young-families cluster, family-living zone and an exclusive villa enclave. Emaar's AED 163.4B Q1 2026 revenue backlog and Dubai Holding's 29.73% strategic ownership underwrite the scale; a phased rollout with EOI and broker briefings is expected once the full unveil drops.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
A single AED 200B masterplan exceeds the combined AED 75B Dubai greenlit across all developers in the first five months of 2026 — it materially resets the city's 2027-2030 supply curve. For sellers in adjacent Downtown, Dubai Hills and Creek Harbour communities, launch pricing will put direct tension on resale comps once disclosed.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Off-plan buyers tracking flagship developer launches, brokers running ultra-luxury villa mandates, corporate occupiers eyeing fresh Grade-A inventory, and resale owners in adjacent Emaar/Meraas master communities reading exit-path pressure.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">AHS drops AED 1.1B on the 42-floor Shangri-La SZR tower and teases an AED 25B Water Canal mega-launch — Dubai's first prime hospitality trade of 2026 prints +57% over the 2020 auction.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
AHS Properties (Abbas Sajwani, 26) acquired the 42-43-floor Shangri-La Hotel on Sheikh Zayed Road from Abu Dhabi-based Mismak Asset Management for AED 1.1B — a +57% premium over the 2020 distressed-auction price. Shangri-La Group retains hotel management; AHS simultaneously disclosed a Q3 2026 launch of 'AHS City', a new AED 25B mixed-use plot on the Dubai Water Canal, pushing portfolio GDV toward AED 50B by year-end.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The AED 1.1B clip prints at roughly AED 1,100/sqft on a ~1M sqft mixed-use GBA (hotel, serviced apartments, offices, F&B) — the first prime SZR hospitality trade since the US-Iran regional flare-up. AHS City on SZR/Water Canal will open an EOI and broker-briefing window within 6-8 weeks of the Q3 launch announcement.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is a live data-point that institutional capital is still clearing trophy SZR assets at peak-corridor pricing in a cooling cycle — Sajwani's 'no more land on Sheikh Zayed Road' scarcity thesis now has a 1M-sqft trade behind it. AHS City will set fresh ceilings for SZR off-plan office and branded-residence pricing in H2 2026.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
SZR/Downtown/Water Canal trophy-asset owners re-marking holdings, institutional buyers underwriting prime Dubai hospitality and mixed-use, brokers chasing AHS City primary mandates, and hotel sponsors comparing Mismak-style exits.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Majid Al Futtaim invokes force-majeure on Tilal Al Ghaf SPAs — Stonehaven flags ~25% input-cost jump, MAF says construction stays on schedule</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Tilal Al Ghaf force-majeure notice flips the off-plan buyer-protection switch — and re-prices delay risk across every comparable Dubai SPA.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Semafor reports Majid Al Futtaim has begun sending force-majeure notifications to 'some customers' at Tilal Al Ghaf, citing post-February-2026 Iran-war supply-chain disruption. MAF disputes the framing and says construction continues in line with its SPA terms — but if the notice holds, it suspends the standard 6-12 month delay-compensation rails Dubai off-plan buyers rely on.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Stonehaven estimates Gulf building-material costs are up ~25% since Sep 2025 (aluminium, bitumen, copper, nickel) and Jebel-Ali-routed cargo is delayed, with subcontractors withdrawing from new work. Standard Dubai SPAs give buyers compensation if handover slips 6-12 months past the registered date — force-majeure invocation can pause that clock entirely.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Every Dubai off-plan buyer underwriting a 2026-27 handover needs to re-read the force-majeure clause in their SPA and request a written status from their developer. Sellers of partially-paid contracts should expect harder secondary-buyer questions on Oqood status and remaining payment exposure.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Tilal Al Ghaf off-plan buyers, secondary-market investors holding any Dubai 2026-27 off-plan SPA, brokers handling assignments, and lawyers structuring delay-compensation claims.
</p>
