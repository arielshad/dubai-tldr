<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Emaar teases AED 200B Dubai mega-masterplan — 4.5M sqm GFA, ~150K residents, 5 character zones, 20-minute city</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Emaar's biggest-ever Dubai land bet — AED 200B, 150K residents, 4.5M sqm across five zones — teased ahead of an imminent full unveil.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Emaar's founder Mohamed Alabbar pre-announced its 'most extraordinary' masterplan yet — an integrated AED 200B (~USD 54.4B) urban district with five zones, swimmable lagoons, a grand boulevard, retail high street, schools, healthcare and cultural spaces. Project name and location remain under wraps, but residences will have views of Burj Khalifa, Burj Al Arab and Palm Jumeirah.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The masterplan spans >4.5M sqm GFA for ~150,000 residents across a business hub, urban district, young-families cluster, family-living zone and an exclusive villa enclave with 5–6 BR villas and mansions. Emaar's Q1 2026 AED 163.4B revenue backlog and Dubai Holding's 29.73% ownership underwrite the scale; a phased rollout with EOI and launch pricing is expected once the full unveil drops.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
A single AED 200B masterplan exceeds the AED 75B total of all 250 Dubai projects greenlit in the first five months of 2026, materially resetting the city's 2027–2030 supply curve. For sellers in adjacent Emaar communities (Downtown, Dubai Hills, Creek Harbour), it places pricing tension on resale stock once launch prices go public.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Off-plan buyers tracking flagship developer launches, brokers running ultra-luxury villa mandates, commercial occupiers eyeing fresh Grade-A inventory, and resale owners in adjacent Emaar masterplans reading their exit-path pressure.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">AHS Properties × Mismak: AED 1.1B Shangri-La Hotel SZR takeover — +57% over 2020, AED 25B 'AHS City' Dubai Water Canal launch teed up for Q3</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">AHS Properties drops AED 1.1B on the 42-floor Shangri-La SZR tower and teases an AED 25B Water Canal mega-launch — Dubai's first prime hospitality trade of 2026 prints +57% over the 2020 auction.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
AHS Properties (Abbas Sajwani, 26) confirmed the AED 1.1B acquisition of the Shangri-La Hotel on Sheikh Zayed Road from Abu Dhabi-based Mismak Asset Management — a 42-43-floor, ~200m-tall mixed-use tower completed 2003 spanning ~1M sqft GBA. Shangri-La Group retains hotel management, so guest operations continue uninterrupted.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The AED 1.1B clip prints +57% above the 2020 AED 700.2M online-auction sale — a 7.8% CAGR through the Covid trough and 2024-25 boom. AHS also disclosed 'AHS City', a new AED 25B mixed-use plot on the Dubai Water Canal / SZR launching Q3 2026, pushing its portfolio GDV toward AED 50B by year-end.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the first prime SZR hospitality trade of 2026 and the largest hotel-anchored mixed-use deal in Dubai since the US-Iran flare-up. For prime owners, it proves institutional capital is still clearing trophy assets at peak-corridor pricing; for brokers, the AED 25B AHS City teaser opens a fresh primary mandate window with EOI and briefings expected within 6–8 weeks.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
SZR / Downtown / Water Canal trophy-asset owners re-marking, institutional capital underwriting prime Dubai hospitality, brokers chasing AHS City primary mandates, and hotel sponsors comparing Mismak-style exits.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Majid Al Futtaim invokes force-majeure on Tilal Al Ghaf buyers — Stonehaven flags ~25% input-cost jump since Sep; MAF says construction stays on schedule</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Tilal Al Ghaf force-majeure notice flips the off-plan buyer-protection switch — and re-prices delay risk across every comparable Dubai SPA.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Semafor reports MAF has begun notifying 'some customers' at Tilal Al Ghaf of force-majeure notifications under their SPAs, citing post-2026-02-28 Iran-war supply-chain disruption. MAF publicly disputes the framing and says construction tracks the schedule and SPA terms. Tilal Al Ghaf is one of Dubai's largest active masterplans, mixing >USD 15M mansions with investor-grade units.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Standard Dubai off-plan SPAs give buyers compensation if handover slips 6–12 months past the registered date — invoking force majeure can pause that clock. Stonehaven estimates Gulf building-material costs are up ~25% since Sep 2025 (aluminium, bitumen, copper, nickel) and state energy companies set the macro precedent by declaring force majeure in Mar 2026.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
If MAF's notice holds, every Dubai off-plan buyer underwriting a 2026–27 handover needs to re-read the force-majeure clause in their SPA and ask the developer for written status. For investors relying on rental income from the handover date, the timeline risk is now explicit; sellers of partially-paid contracts should expect harder questions from secondary buyers about Oqood status.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Tilal Al Ghaf off-plan buyers, secondary-market investors holding any Dubai 2026–27 off-plan SPA, brokers handling assignments, and lawyers structuring delay-compensation claims.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Naia Island's third beachfront plot of 2026 — AED 167M Middle Eastern end-user — completes a AED 1B two-month tally via Dubai Sotheby's.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The National reports Shamal Holding's Naia Island has booked AED 1B (USD 272M) across three beachfront-plot trades in two months. This week's AED 167M sale to a Middle Eastern buyer joins the AED 560M (May) and AED 377M (April) plots — all closed through Dubai Sotheby's International Realty, all to end-users per Sotheby's MEA chairman George Azar.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The cluster sits along the Jumeirah-coast Naia Island master anchored by a Cheval Blanc Maison Hotel and private-villa programme. With ~9,800 relocating millionaires entering the UAE in 2025 and Dubai booking ~500 $10M+ home sales per year, the Naia bid is concentrated, end-user and held outside the trade-flow.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
AED 1B in two months on one master is a clean read on Dubai super-prime land scarcity during a regional-war window. The all-end-user buyer profile removes the assignment-resale ceiling and supports trophy-asset thesis underwrites for adjacent Jumeirah-coast and Palm-Jebel-Ali plays.
