<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Dubai Pearl / Al Sufouh Mega-Brownfield Restarts Under Dubai Holding</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">AGBI catches Dubai Pearl restarting — Al Sufouh's 163,000 sqm brownfield is back in Dubai Holding hands (Dham Property Group) with a 900,000 sqm GFA envelope and 173,000 sqm of hospitality baked in.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
AGBI confirms the former Dubai Pearl site at Al Sufouh — at the base of Palm Jumeirah — is moving again. The plot is registered to Dham Property Group, a Dubai Holding subsidiary since 2022, for a mixed-use scheme up to 900,000 sqm GFA across a 66-storey envelope with Aecom on services.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The permitted programme spans apartments, offices, retail, hotel plus hotel apartments, clubhouse, nursery, kindergarten and clinic. Hospitality alone exceeds 173,000 sqm of permitted GFA. DDA plans currently expire November 2027; the site has been cleared since early 2024 after controlled demolitions.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is one of two brownfield mega-plots on the Palm Jumeirah / Al Sufouh axis. Bringing 900,000 sqm of GFA back onto Al Sufouh with a Jumeirah-brand hospitality anchor reshapes the medium-term supply pipeline for prime-residential and hotel-apartment stock adjacent to Palm Jumeirah and DIC.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Luxury-agency brokers scoping Palm Jumeirah / Al Sufouh branded-residence pre-launch pipeline; family offices tracking Dubai Holding mega-plot roadmap; hospitality operators reading a &gt;173,000 sqm hotel + branded-residence envelope.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Julius Baer Drops Dubai to 14th — Dirham Peg Optical Move, Real Estate Still #1 ME HNWI Asset Class</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Julius Baer drops Dubai seven rungs to 14th on its luxury-lifestyle index — a dollar-peg optical move, not a local price cut — with prime real estate still the #1 ME HNWI asset class at 23%.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
The Julius Baer 2026 Global Wealth &amp; Lifestyle Report puts Dubai at 14th in its cross-city luxury-lifestyle cost index, down from 7th last year. Singapore holds #1 for a fourth year; Zurich takes #2 and Monaco enters the top three for the first time. Real estate is called out as the #1 preferred asset class for Middle East HNWIs at 23%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
The report converts a 25-city basket of luxury spend into USD; Dubai's dirham is pegged to a weakening dollar, so the emirate looks cheaper in USD even as local pricing holds. Global luxury goods rose 12.3% on average (jewellery +16.4%, watches +15.5%). One-third of ME HNWIs report significant wealth accumulation; 43% lifted both investment and lifestyle spend.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Dropping seven places is a positioning headline, not a repricing — it reinforces the pitch to internationally mobile buyers who compare Dubai against Zurich, Monaco, Hong Kong and Singapore in USD terms. The dirham peg is now an explicit brochure line for prime-residential agents targeting European and Asian HNWIs.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Luxury agencies pitching Palm Jumeirah / Emirates Hills / Jumeirah Bay to European and Asian HNWIs; branded-residence sales teams framing the currency angle; family offices comparing Dubai prime-residential to Zurich, Monaco, Hong Kong and Singapore.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">AVENEW × KORA Launch O1NE District at Motor City — AED 6B Six-Tower Grade A Masterplan</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Motor City gets its first AED 6B commercial masterplan — a six-tower Grade A office district anchored by DAWN with a retail spine and a healthcare facility.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
AVENEW Development and KORA Properties (APPCORP Holding / Apparel Group) unveil O1NE District — a mixed-use commercial masterplan next to the Dubai Autodrome. Phase one opens with DAWN, a Grade A office tower with flexible floorplates and direct air-conditioned links to the retail spine. Five more commercial towers, a retail destination and a healthcare facility follow.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Sales for DAWN commenced 2026-07-08 as the anchor of an AED 6B / USD 1.6B masterplan. The district sits adjacent to the Autodrome with routes to Downtown Dubai, Palm Jumeirah and future Al Maktoum International Airport. Amenities include a concierge super lobby, landscaped podium terraces, sky gardens, rooftop dining and outdoor workspaces.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Motor City's commercial stock has been rental-dominated and mid-grade — an AED 6B Grade A masterplan repositions it as a commercial district for Business Bay overspill and Expo-corridor tenants. Six towers plus retail and healthcare sets a new ask/sqft benchmark for whole-plate office demand in DubaiLand.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Occupiers, corporate real-estate teams and investors underwriting Grade A office plays in DubaiLand / Al Khail; brokers matching tenants priced out of Business Bay or DIFC; healthcare and retail operators scouting anchor space.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">DLD's Tamallak+ Wins Hamdan Flag — 88% Faster Service, 5-Minute Property Sales</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">DLD's Tamallak+ takes the 2025 Hamdan Flag with the receipts — 88% faster service, 5-minute property sales, 59 developers + 30 banks wired in and 96.5% CSAT.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Dubai Land Department wins the 2025 Hamdan Flag in the Pioneering category for its Tamallak+ platform — a digital stack layering AI-driven property valuation, instant sales registration, developer self-registration, first-time-buyer support and real-estate asset tokenization onto Dubai's property-transfer flow.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Tamallak+ has cut service completion times 88% by integrating 59 major developers and 30 banks into a single flow, so some property sales register in as little as five minutes with no in-person visit. Customer satisfaction sits at 96.5% and DLD attributes 129,000+ new investors in 2025 partly to the friction reduction.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>

</p>
