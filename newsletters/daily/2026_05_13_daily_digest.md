<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">ICD transfers its entire 22.27% Emaar Properties stake to Dubai Holding's Emirates Power Investment — Dubai Holding consolidates to 29.73% as Emaar's largest shareholder</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Dubai Inc. reshuffles Emaar's cap table: sovereign wealth fund ICD hands its full 22.27% stake to Dubai Holding's Emirates Power Investment, consolidating control under Sheikh Mohammed's investment vehicle.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
On 11 May 2026 the Investment Corporation of Dubai — Dubai's sovereign wealth fund, ninth-largest globally with $429B in AUM — transferred its entire 22.2723% shareholding in Emaar Properties (DFM: EMAAR) to Emirates Power Investment, a wholly-owned Dubai Holding subsidiary. Dubai Holding's combined group stake now reaches 29.73%, making it Emaar's largest single shareholder; ICD retains zero Emaar shares.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
This is an intra-government corporate restructuring, not an open-market block trade — both ICD and Dubai Holding sit under Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum. It mirrors ICD's earlier 2024 transfer of Emirates NBD shares to Dubai Holding, a pattern of consolidating sector-aligned holdings while ICD refocuses on diversification toward its $602B by-2030 AUM target.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Free float and listing status are unchanged, but the largest shareholder is now Dubai Holding (which owns Meraas, Dubai Properties, Nakheel-adjacent JVs and the new Brookfield JV) rather than the sovereign wealth fund — making it a kingmaker on dividend policy, capital allocation and any future Emaar-Meraas-Dubai Properties consolidation.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
EMAAR.AE shareholders, Dubai-listed equity allocators, off-plan buyers tracking developer governance, and M&A watchers monitoring a potential Emaar-Meraas-Dubai Properties consolidation play.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Emaar Q1 2026: AED 20.1B property sales (+22%), AED 3.5B net profit (+36%), AED 134.6B backlog as 10 new launches land before regional conflict spreads</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">The biggest Dubai developer printed AED 20B of sales in a quarter that ended with a regional war — backlog now AED 134.6B and 10 new launches in flight.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Emaar Properties Q1 2026: sales at Emaar Development were AED 20.1B (+22% YoY); group revenue AED 12.4B (+23%); net profit ~AED 3.5B (+36%); revenue backlog AED 134.6B (+35%). The company also paid out AED 8.9B (100% of share capital) at its AGM and launched 10 new residential projects across master-planned communities.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
AED 20.1B in Q1 sales is roughly AED 6.7B per month — well ahead of Q1 2025. The AED 134.6B backlog implies ~6.7 quarters of forward revenue, with 10 new launches plugging into existing master communities (Dubai Hills, Creek Harbour, Emaar South, Beachfront, Downtown, Arabian Ranches III).
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Emaar is the single biggest developer-side benchmark in Dubai; Q1 sales up 22% and backlog up 35% during a quarter that ended with a regional conflict demolishes the "launches will dry up" thesis. For resale sellers in Emaar communities, they now compete against fresh-launch payment plans, not just other resale ads.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Off-plan brokers tracking primary-market depth; resale sellers in Emaar communities benchmarking against new-launch pricing; institutional analysts modelling Dubai supply 2027–28.
</p>

<h2 style="margin: 0 0 8px; font-size: 24px; font-weight: 800; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.2;">Binghatti Q1 2026: AED 1.43B net profit (+73%), AED 5.88B sales across 4,000+ units, AED 52B backlog as 5 launches and $500M sukuk land in the quarter</h2>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Second Dubai developer print of the day after Emaar: Binghatti clears AED 1.43B net profit on AED 5.88B sales — proof off-plan demand is broad, not Emaar-only.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Binghatti — Dubai's biggest pure off-plan developer by unit count — posted Q1 2026 net profit of AED 1.43B (+73% YoY) on AED 4.39B revenue (+52%), with AED 5.88B in sales across 4,000+ units. Five new launches added AED 8.58B / 4,696 units to the pipeline; a $500M 5.5-year sukuk was placed 4.4x oversubscribed — the longest-tenor sukuk from a UAE private real-estate developer.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Development backlog stands at AED 52B, with AED 16B of sales backlog and AED 18B of revenue backlog yet to recognise. Net margin expanded to 33% (vs 29% in Q1 2025) and cash on balance sheet is AED 9.9B against AED 32.87B total assets.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
Pairs with Emaar's same-day print: two top-five off-plan developers both beating expectations argues Q1 off-plan dominance (76% of value) is structural, not concentrated. The AED 52B backlog is a forward indicator of handover supply in 2027–2029.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Off-plan buyers tracking developer solvency and delivery risk; institutional credit investors; primary-market underwriters benchmarking Q1 2026 absorption versus Emaar, DAMAC, and Sobha.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Independent tracker logs AED 1.7B of Dubai asking-price cuts — secondary villas leading, off-plan a step behind.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
AGBI's 11 May report cites LuxuryPriceDrops.com, which monitors ~27,000 live Dubai listings, finding 2,800+ properties marked down a cumulative AED 1.7B ($463M) since the regional conflict started in late February, with 500+ new drops logged in the past week alone.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Secondary (ready) markdowns are running roughly 2× off-plan markdowns by count. The single biggest active cut is a 4-bedroom Jumeirah villa repriced from AED 110M to AED 85M (−23%); an Arabian Ranches villa has been cut five times from AED 8.5M to AED 7.4M and is still unsold.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
This is the most credible third-party measurement of seller motivation in Dubai right now, giving buyers a price-discovery anchor for negotiating off list prices in Jumeirah villas, Arabian Ranches, and mid-luxury Marina/Downtown stock. DLD March 2026 residential sales were AED 37B — down ~20%, the steepest monthly drop since the pandemic.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Cash buyers hunting villa repricings, brokers running buy-side mandates, secondary sellers benchmarking their ask against where the market is actually clearing.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #8a8a85; line-height: 1.5;">Property Finder/Mortgage Finder: March's panic was a deferral, not a departure — April mortgages hit AED 9B and 7 lenders had cut LTV.</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">What is it?</strong><br>
Property Finder's 12-May release: April closed at AED 9.02B in DLD-registered mortgages — a 2026 high — after a March shock where 10% of Mortgage Finder clients cancelled, 20% paused, and seven mainstream lenders cut LTV from 80% to 70%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">How does it work?</strong><br>
Investor purchase intent on Mortgage Finder rose ~4x between March and April. Villa searches hit 46% of all sale searches — the year's high — while rental contract volumes were +16% YoY in April. Off-plan primary held +20% YoY in March even as secondary slid 34%.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 16px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Why does it matter?</strong><br>
The LTV cut from 80% to 70% at seven lenders means buyer cash requirements jumped during March — confirm with your lender whether the cut has reversed before underwriting any deal that needs &gt;70% LTV. Mortgage Finder intent and DLD mortgage filings are now leading indicators worth checking before pricing a deal.
</p>

<p style="margin: 0 0 20px; font-size: 15px; color: #f5f5f0; line-height: 1.6;">
<strong style="color: #f7ff00;">Who is it for?</strong><br>
Mortgage brokers, originators, buyer-side agents, and underwriters needing to re-set LTV assumptions before pricing or committing to any 2026 transaction.
</p>
